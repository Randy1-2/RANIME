Best Animation

Year Winner & Length Technical Achievement
2025 Demon Slayer: Hashira Training Arc Studio: ufotable Redefined the "Training Arc" trope with cinematic lighting and hyper-fluid sword forms during the final sequence.
2024 Demon Slayer: Swordsmith Village Arc Studio: ufotable Showcased groundbreaking 3D-model integration for the Upper Moon demons and complex environmental particle effects.
2023 Demon Slayer: Entertainment District Arc Studio: ufotable Widely cited as a TV animation peak for its high-octane destruction physics and dynamic camera movements in the final battle.
2022 Demon Slayer: Mugen Train Arc Studio: ufotable Brought theatrical-level rendering and digital compositing to the weekly television format.
2021 Jujutsu Kaisen Studio: MAPPA Fascinated audiences with gritty, weight-heavy combat choreography and unique, artistic "Domain Expansion" visuals.
2020 Demon Slayer: Kimetsu no Yaiba Studio: ufotable The breakout year for the "Ukiyo-e" breathing effects, merging traditional Japanese art styles with modern CGI.
2019 Devilman Crybaby Studio: Science SARU Won for its avant-garde, fluid, and "rubber-hose" style animation directed by Masaaki Yuasa.
2018 Made in Abyss Studio: Kinema Citrus Distinguished by its hand-painted, lush backgrounds and the unsettlingly detailed creature animation.
2017 Yuri!!! on Ice Studio: MAPPA Recognized for the incredible difficulty of rotoscoping and animating complex figure skating routines.

Best Drama

Year Winner & Length Narrative Legacy
2025 Frieren: Beyond Journey's End Studio: Madhouse A melancholic, beautifully paced exploration of time, regret, and the legacy of companionship.

2017 Erased Studio: A-1 Pictures The first-ever winner in this category; a tight, mystery-driven drama about changing the past to save a future.
